Meat Rolls


Meat rolls are a delicious and versatile dish that can be enjoyed as a main course or as a snack. They are made by wrapping seasoned meat in a dough or pastry, then baking or frying until golden brown. The filling can vary widely, from minced beef or pork to chicken or even vegetables, making them suitable for various tastes and dietary preferences.

These rolls are often served with dipping sauces or gravies, enhancing their flavor and making them even more enjoyable. They are perfect for parties, gatherings, or a comforting family meal. Whether you prefer them spicy, savory, or with a hint of sweetness, meat rolls are sure to satisfy your cravings.

Ingredients

  • 500g minced meat (beef, pork, or chicken)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 package of pastry dough or bread rolls
  • Oil for frying or baking

Steps

  • Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F) if baking.
  • In a skillet, heat some oil and sauté the chopped onion and minced garlic until translucent.
  • Add the minced meat to the skillet, season with salt, pepper, paprika, and soy sauce. Cook until the meat is browned and cooked through.
  • Roll out the pastry dough or prepare the bread rolls.
  • Place a portion of the meat mixture onto each piece of dough or bread, then roll them up tightly.
  • If baking, place the rolls on a baking sheet and brush with oil. Bake for about 20-25 minutes or until golden brown.
  • If frying, heat oil in a pan and fry the rolls until they are golden and crispy on all sides.
  • Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce or gravy.
