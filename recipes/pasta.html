Pasta


Pasta is a staple food of Italian cuisine, made from wheat flour and water, and sometimes eggs. It comes in various shapes and sizes, such as spaghetti, penne, fusilli, and lasagna. Pasta can be served with a wide range of sauces, from simple olive oil and garlic to rich tomato or cream-based sauces. It is versatile and can be paired with vegetables, meats, or seafood, making it a favorite dish worldwide.

Whether enjoyed in a hearty Bolognese, a light Aglio e Olio, or a creamy Carbonara, pasta is loved for its comforting texture and ability to absorb flavors. It can be cooked al dente for a firmer bite or simmered longer for a softer texture. Pasta dishes are often garnished with cheese, herbs, or spices to enhance their flavor.


Ingredients

  • 200g pasta (spaghetti, penne, or your choice)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (400g) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Steps

  • Boil a pot of salted water and cook the pasta according to package instructions until al dente.
  • In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and saut√© until fragrant.
  • Add the diced tomatoes and oregano to the skillet. Season with salt and pepper. Simmer for about 10 minutes.
  • Drain the cooked pasta and add it to the skillet, tossing to combine with the sauce.
  • Serve hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves and grated Parmesan cheese if desired.
